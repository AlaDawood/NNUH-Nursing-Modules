Caring For Your Ears

 

  • Wax is normal – it protects and lubricates the Ear Canal.

 

  • It is a mixture of Cerumen (a sweat-like substance) and Sebum (an oily substance) both secreted by glands in your Ear Canal, which combines with dust, debris, hair etc to form Ear Wax. It is normal to have Ear Wax, and it usually only becomes a problem when it has been pushed deeper into the Ear Canal.

 

  • Your ears are self cleaning – if you use Cotton Wool buds or similar objects, to try to remove the wax, it will only be pushed further into the Ear Canal and become impacted and block your ear.

 

  • Using Olive Oil ear drops (2 drops once a week) may prevent wax from blocking your ears.

 

  • If you have ear problems such as dry skin, eczema, dermatitis, “runny ears”, you will find keeping your ears DRY will improve the condition. Avoid shampoo, shower gel and hairspray etc in your ears.

HOW TO USE EAR DROPS

 

Using Olive Oil Ear Drops

 

Olive Oil encourages the natural movement of wax from the outer ear. It is advisable to use a “dropper” to instil the Olive Oil and these can be purchased from a pharmacy, who will sell “Olive Oil Eardrops” which come complete with a dropper.

 

Most patients find that applying 1-2 drops at night, for 5-7 nights prior to an appointment for ear irrigation, is sufficient to soften the wax for comfortable irrigation. If you have a continual problem with ear wax, or itchy ears, it may be beneficial to apply 1-2 drops of Olive Oil weekly. This does not stop wax from building up, but makes the natural movement of wax from the ear canal more efficient.

 

How to Apply Ear Drops Effectively

 

  • Lie down on your side with the affected ear upwards
  • Gently pull the outer ear upwards and outwards. Using room temperature Olive Oil and the dropper drop 1-2 drops into the ear canal and gently massage the area in front of the ear.

 

 

  • Remain lying down for 10 minutes and then wipe away any excess oil. DO NOT PUT COTTON WOOL IN THE EAR as this will absorb the oil.
  • Repeat the procedure with the opposite ear if required.
  • Any fluid that enters your ear canal can make wax swell and temporary diminish hearing, so we advise irrigating one ear at a time.
